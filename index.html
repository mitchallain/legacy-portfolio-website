Internship with R3 Sciences, Enersciences, LLC., Summer of 2014

In the spring of 2014, Enersciences, LLC selected me to participate as an intern in their research division. Scientists there were looking to explore the use of a low temperature plasma reactor to catalyze certain chemical pathways, and perhaps discover a novel dissociation process for industry hazards such as fugitive methane emissions or carbon dioxide gases. My two-man team dealt daily with high- voltage power sources and diagnostic tools. We quantified plasma generation with circuit analysis techniques from relevant literature. In less than three months, we demonstrated a functioning plasma reactor. We also implemented the necessary diagnostics and prepared the research to be continued by staff chemists.
